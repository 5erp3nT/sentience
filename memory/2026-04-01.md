### [02:18:23] ASSISTANT
**Z‑Image‑Turbo** is a distilled version of the Z‑Image model, trimmed to roughly **2 B parameters**. In FP16 it uses about **6‑7 GB VRAM**, so it fits comfortably on an 8 GB GPU. If you quantize to 4‑bit (or use 8‑bit inference) the memory drops to **≈4 GB**, leaving extra headroom for the VAE and UI.

**Key points**

- **Size:** ~2 B params → ~6 GB VRAM (FP16) or ~4 GB (4‑bit).  
- **Speed:** ~2‑3× faster than the full 6 B Z‑Image checkpoint because the UNet is shallower.  
- **Quality:** Slightly lower fidelity than the full model, but still comparable to Stable Diffusion 1.5 for most prompts.  
- **Setup:** Load with `bitsandbytes` or `auto‑gptq` for 4‑bit; enable `torch.compile` for extra speed.

So, for an 8 GB GPU, **Z‑Image‑Turbo** is a strong candidate—offering a good trade‑off between speed, quality, and memory usage. If you need even lower VRAM, you can further quantize to 8‑bit or use CPU off‑loading, though that will slow inference.
